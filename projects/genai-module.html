Educator Training

Mastering GenAI for Learning Design

A self-paced module that helps educators harness generative AI to build complete, thoughtful lesson plans -- without losing the pedagogy.

Role: Instructional Designer Context: GW -- EDUC 6427 Audience: Educators and Trainers Format: Web-based, Self-Paced
Try the Module
The Need

Students adopted AI faster than their teachers did.

The growing use of GenAI tools like ChatGPT in classrooms, trainings, and meetings has created a skills gap. Students and employees often adopt these tools faster than their educators or supervisors, leading to a disconnect. Many facilitators are unsure how to use AI without sacrificing learning quality, equity, or academic integrity.

Most professional development currently lacks clear frameworks for designing objectives and assessments with AI support, explicit equity adaptations, real-time low-stakes experimentation, and flexible tools that work across diverse instructional environments. Educators don't need to be told AI exists. They need a structured workflow that preserves their professional judgment.

The Module

60 minutes from skeptic to skilled.

This self-paced, 60-minute course equips instructional designers, teachers, coaches, and other facilitators with hands-on tools to use generative AI responsibly and effectively when planning instruction. Navigation is non-linear: learners can enter through any module and work in any order.

The module focuses on four core units: SMART Objectives, Assessments, Instructional Strategies, and Centering All Participants (anti-racist pedagogy). Each unit contains four repeatable components: an introduction with narration, educational theory grounding, a real-time prompt builder with live input, and a knowledge check.

The audience is broad by design -- classroom teachers, corporate trainers, coaches, and team leads. No prior knowledge of AI, pedagogy jargon, or specific LMS systems is required. The module is published on a responsive web platform with no login required, removing every adoption barrier possible.

Four Core Modules

Non-linear, repeatable, practical.

Each module follows the same structure: intro with narration, educational theory grounding, a real-time prompt builder with live input, and a knowledge check. Learners can enter through any module and work in any order.

Module 1: SMART Objectives

Crafting clear, measurable learning objectives that guide AI prompting and ensure generated content aligns with instructional goals -- the foundation everything else builds on.

Module 2: Assessments

Aligning assessments to objectives before generating instructional activities. Backward design principles applied to AI-assisted planning, ensuring coherence from the start.

Module 3: Instructional Strategies

Using the prompt builder to generate pedagogically rich instructional activities -- moving beyond generic AI outputs to plans with real depth, engagement structures, and formative checkpoints.

Module 4: Center All Participants

Anti-racist and equity-centered pedagogy applied to AI-generated plans. Adapting for IEP, 504, ESL, and gifted populations -- ensuring AI doesn't flatten the diversity of learners.

Real-Time Prompt Builder

Every module includes a live prompt builder where learners input real content and get immediate AI-generated results -- then practice revision, critique, and refinement on the spot.

Open Access Platform

Published on a responsive web platform with no login, no paywall, and no LMS dependency. Accessible to any educator with a browser -- from a DC classroom to a corporate training room.

Design Philosophy

Built with the R3 Model.

Traditional step-by-step instructional design models wouldn't work for the fast, creative process this project demanded. So I created my own model as I went: the R3 Design Model -- Real-Time, Real-World, Responsive. It reflects how I actually built the product: with constant adjustments, multiple goals in mind, and a real-world audience of testers providing feedback as I went.

R3 has seven stages: identify the gap and audience, set the vision, design in condensed connected modules, build and test simultaneously, refine with GenAI support, accept imperfection and prioritize impact, and organize late but well. I didn't wait until everything was finished to get feedback -- I built as I tested, asking a diverse group (market researchers, swim coaches, high school teachers) to go through it and share reactions in real time.

This module was both designed using R3 and designed to teach its principles. Educators don't just learn to use AI -- they learn to iterate on what AI produces, test it against learner needs, and refine it until it meets a standard of quality that reflects their professional expertise. AI integration isn't about replacing teacher judgment. It's about giving educators faster paths to the kind of plans they would build anyway, while maintaining full control over pedagogical decisions.
