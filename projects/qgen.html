Data Infrastructure

QGen

A full-stack survey analysis and project management platform that transformed how an entire firm handles market research.

Role: Sole Designer and Developer Company: Cue Insights Timeline: 2024 -- Present Stack: Next.js, Supabase, Python, Docker
The Problem

Manual processes don't scale.

At Cue Insights, every market research project followed the same pattern: build questionnaires by hand in Word, validate SPSS data files by manually checking variable labels, calculate incidence rates in spreadsheets, and generate crosstabs through a patchwork of tools. Qualitative coding meant a researcher spending hours reading open-ended responses and tagging themes one by one.

The firm needed a single platform that could handle the entire project lifecycle -- from questionnaire construction to final deliverables -- while eliminating the manual bottlenecks that slowed every project down.

The Solution

One platform, end to end.

QGen is a company-wide web application that I designed and built from scratch. It consolidates the entire survey research workflow into a single, accessible platform: project intake, questionnaire generation, data validation, statistical computation, visualization, and AI-assisted qualitative analysis.

The platform replaced fragmented processes with a unified system that any team member can use, regardless of technical skill level. It doesn't just automate tasks -- it restructures how the firm thinks about research operations.

Core Capabilities

What QGen does.

Questionnaire Generation

Automates the construction of survey instruments from project specifications, ensuring consistency across screener logic, question flow, and response options.

SPSS Data Validation

Ingests raw SPSS data files and systematically validates variable labels, value assignments, skip patterns, and data integrity before any analysis begins.

Incidence Rate Calculation

Computes incidence rates and qualification metrics automatically, giving project managers real-time visibility into fieldwork progress and feasibility.

Data Visualization

Generates presentation-ready charts, graphs, and visual summaries directly from validated data -- formatted for client deliverables without manual design work.

Crosstab Generation

Produces cross-tabulation tables with statistical testing, banner points, and significance markers -- the core deliverable format in market research.

AI Qualitative Coding

Uses AI to analyze open-ended survey responses, identifying themes and generating codebooks that replaced hours of manual qualitative coding across the firm.

My Role

Everything, from scratch.

I was the sole designer and developer for QGen. That meant every decision -- from the database schema to the UI component library to the Python processing pipeline -- was mine to make.

I identified the operational bottlenecks through direct experience working on research projects. I architected the platform, chose the tech stack, built the frontend and backend, wrote the data processing logic, integrated AI capabilities for qualitative coding, containerized the application with Docker, and deployed it for the entire company.

Beyond the technical work, I managed the rollout: training team members, gathering feedback, and iterating on the platform based on real-world use across diverse research projects in healthcare and pharmaceutical markets.

Reflection

What this taught me.

Building QGen reinforced something I already believed: the structures we create around work shape the quality of what gets produced. A researcher using QGen doesn't just work faster -- they work differently. When the friction of manual validation and formatting disappears, the intellectual energy goes back to where it belongs: analysis, interpretation, and insight.

That principle is the same one behind my curriculum research. Whether the context is a classroom or a firm, the systems and structures we build around people determine what kind of thinking they're able to do. QGen is Axiomatica applied to market research.
