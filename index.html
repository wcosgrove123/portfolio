The purpose of school should not be focused on what to learn. It should be centered on how to learn.

From "Same Means, New End: A Metacognitive End for American Curriculum"

I'm finishing my MA in Curriculum Design and Pedagogy at George Washington University, where my thesis makes a research-grounded case for reorganizing K-12 education around how students think rather than around prescribed content. Before grad school, I taught AP US History and AP Government. I currently work as a Project Director at a market research firm. What connects all of it is a simple conviction: the way we teach matters as much as what we teach.
