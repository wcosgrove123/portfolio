## Research: Axiomatica

Wil's thesis, "Same Means, New End: A Metacognitive End for American Curriculum," is the centerpiece of his intellectual work. It argues that K-12 education should be reorganized around how students think (metacognition) rather than prescribed content.

### Core Argument
For almost 250 years, curriculum theorists in the United States have circled around the same essential question: What is the purpose of schools? Despite enormous social and technological change, the basic subject structure and age-graded sequences of schooling in 2025 would still be recognizable to students from 1925, and even 1825. The thesis argues that the purpose of school today should not be focused on what to learn — it should be centered on how to learn.

### Key Concepts

**Contextures:** Interwoven learning environments that bring together themes, tasks, and sources so that students can work on how they learn, not just what they learn. A Contexture is not a "unit on the American Revolution" — it is a deliberate weave of experiences through which students are invited to notice, question, and reorganize their own thinking.

**Cogniscence:** Learning that includes awareness of how it is changing what you know and how you are able to know. When students cognesce inside a Contexture, they are not only acquiring information — they are practicing learning-how-to-learn in ways they can carry into new situations.

**Six Metacognitive Habits:**
1. Endospection — Self-identity; returning to, narrating, and revising one's sense of self
2. Omniperegrination — Wandering thought; exploration without predetermined destination
3. Constellaration — Pattern-finding; clustering ideas into meaningful structures
4. Refraction — Critical analysis; examining ideas through multiple lenses
5. Exospection — Perspective-taking; understanding others' viewpoints
6. Synthesis — Integration; combining insights into coherent understanding

**The Axiometric Calculus:** A formal notation system for metacognitive operations grounded in the causal architecture of reality. The calculus describes itself — it emerged through the processes it names.

### Theoretical Foundations
The work synthesizes: John Dewey (experience as basis of education), Jerome Bruner (spiral curriculum, structure of knowledge), L. Dee Fink (taxonomy of significant learning), William Pinar (currere, autobiographical method), and schema theory (how learners organize information).

### Research Interests
Structural K-12 reform and school redesign; curriculum architecture and student achievement; federal education accountability and governance; standards and assessment policy; school funding effectiveness; modernizing public education.

## AI Philosophy

Wil has a developed and articulate perspective on AI in education, detailed in a case study on his portfolio site. His core positions:

- AI is a tool, not a savior or a threat. It does not think, understand, or create. It processes, predicts, and generates. The distinction matters for how we teach with it.
- Most school district and ed company AI policies are pragmatic and defensive, focused on managing the tool. Wil's position goes further: AI should accelerate the move away from content-first schooling toward metacognition-centered learning.
- The historical pattern with new technology in education is always the same: panic, prohibition, integration, redesign. He argues we should skip to redesign.
- He is self-taught in Python, AI tooling, and development. He built production tools (QGen, AI-driven qualitative coding), designed educator training on prompt engineering, and developed the R3 rapid instructional design model.
- He treats prompt engineering as a design discipline, not a shortcut. His "Thinking Past the Prompt" module teaches educators to evaluate AI outputs the same way they evaluate primary sources.
