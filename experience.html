Professional Experience

Learning through
doing.

Each role has shaped how I think about systems, people, and the design of learning.

I'll be honest: I don't have years of experience in a single sector of the traditional corporate world. What I have is a lifetime spent observing, experiencing, analyzing, and designing curriculum in every form it takes - private schools, public schools, religious schools, athletic programs, higher education, charter and experimental models, and one-on-one coaching. I've studied curriculum at the highest theoretical level and practiced at the most granular - writing formative assessments for individual students in real classrooms and on pool decks.

I've been a teacher, a market researcher, an entrepreneurial school founder, a charity organizer, and an application developer. Through all of it, I've stayed on the frontier of technology and innovation while grounding my work in the historical analysis I was trained in - because every era believes it has arrived, and a historian knows that none of them have. The only way to design well for the present is by learning from what came before and preparing for what comes next.

More than any title on this page, my primary role has always been student. I've spent my whole life teaching myself how to learn, and I carry that into every position I take. Even as a teacher, you can only teach by learning.
